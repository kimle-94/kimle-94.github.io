CONTEXT

"Citation Bank" is originally my team submission at Adobe Jam Contest 2019. The topic is to create a mobile app that encourage academic integrity. Within only 3 hours, my teammate and I came to the idea of creating a new app to generate citations and save it at one permanent place.

overview

Citation Bank is the first application that allows users to generate accurate citations and references in a specific style such as APA, MLA, and Chicago and to store them in one
place for later use.

user expectations

- In order to enhance accessibility, users prefer tablets and phones to laptops or paper. In addition, most of these citation sites require a premium membership, which is a deterrent to students.
- Additionally, it is useful and convenient to keep track of references for later use, or even to share with friends.

the problem

- Mosts of users tent to rely more on citation websites than themselves because they find it difficult to remember all the different citation styles.
- Users commonly find references with their tablets, phones, and ipads rather than their laptops.
- Social media, Word, and Google Drive are the most popular options for storing references and citations.

the solution

- Although there are several potential competitors, none of them were able to meet the user needs, especially storaging for references and sharing links with friends. Furthermore, there is no mobile app for creating and saving citations yet.
- "Citation Bank" is a distinctive version - the first mobile app that allows users to generate and save citations with ease.

THE PROJECT RESEARCH

user research

I have conducted two methods of user research interviews with 10 people from various backgrounds and different ages. The key pain points of the users were determined.

online survey

The first user-research survey consisted of five multiple-choice questions to determine the habits and knowledge of interviewees regarding creating citations.

user persona

The second user research was the user persona. Using user persona to represent real users helped me to focus on the goals and needs of users along with to identify their pain points. I was able to identify a problem and develop a solution by better understanding the users.

competitive analysis

After understanding the pain points users had suffered, I looked at other citation websites such as Grammarly, EasyBib, Citation Machine, and citethisforme to identify their strengths and weaknesses, which helped me determine what features should and should not be included in the Citation Bank app.

sitemap

I used a sitemap to determine the layout and organization of each screen. To ensure the user-centered process, it is important to make sure that content can be found where the user is expecting to find it.

task flow

I used a task flow to Identify the most effective way for completing a task with the least amount of friction possible.

lo-fi wireframes

In the beginning, I wanted to design this app with a minimalist style so I went directly to the wireframe without going through the sketches step.

visual design

Inspired by paper books, I want users to feel as if they are reading a book when using the app, especially with the in-text citations. Additionally, the minimal design will make sure that users are able to focus on their main goal when using the app. The typefaces and color palette were chosen to influence attributes such as straightforward, clear, minimal.

key solution

takeaways

The original plan of the project was to design a mobile app that generates citations at Adobe Creative Jam 2019. Due to our time constraints, my teammate and I were unable to conduct the user research; however, I am also an international student, so I understand their frustrations in taking the English course. This is the reason why I decided to completely revamp the project, from UX research to UI design. Especially, this encourages me to be more proactive in getting in touch with people around me, and see what I can do to make their lives better.
