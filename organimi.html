WHAT IS ORGANIMI INC.,?

Organimi is a trendy and affordable market-leading org chart tool for organizational design, people planning, onboarding, and sales and account management.

OUR CHALLENGE

The dashboard design appears outdated compared to its competitors. Organimi needs an upgrade to ensure that it still remains competitive in the long haul.

How might I redesign a dashboard to allow users to manage their files and report on their progress more effectively and easily?

ORIENTATION

Before jumping into redesign the dashboard page, I spent my time to define the purpose and important rules when designing a dashboard because this was my first time. After reading two articles on “10 rules for better dashboard design” by Taras Bakusevych and “Dashboard Design: best practices and examples” by Justinmind , I summarized some key insights I believed that it’s helpful for the Organimi dashboard.

  1. Operational dashboard. It presents data deviations to the users quickly and clearly, show current resource, and display their status. It’s a digital control room designed to help users be quick, proactive, and efficient.
  2. Define the layout and flow. Grids can help to achieve effective alignment and consistency with little effort and create a basic structure or a skeleton for the design.
  3. Follow clear and consistent naming conventions and consistent date formatting. The biggest benefit of using a clear framework is data consistency. If your data is named the same way in each tool, it will be easier for you to use those tools.
  4. Don’t hide information or rely on interactions too much. As one of the primary goals of the dashboard is to surface information at a glance, relying on long scrolling or many interactions dilutes the whole purpose.
  5. Use consistent design language and icon system. Dashboards often contain different styles of data views, so by keeping design light and synchronic, it could deliver clear visibility, simple navigation and striking good looks.

EVALUATION FOR THE OLD DASHBOARD

I took an overview of Organimi's orginal dashboard after taking key insights from the research. I listed 9 things that needed to be redesigned:

  1. The dashboard could be divided into 3 main sections - Sidebard menu, Recent files, and Quick actions. However, the distance from the top bar to Dashboard, Recent Files, and Quick Actions was inconsistent and the text size was not the same (Dashboard: 22px, Recent Files: 24px, and Quick Actions: 28px). This made the white space untidy and obscure to the users.
  2. Name of the organization “Bank of America” needed to be highlighted by another color.
  3. Sidebar Menu was not arranged well due to the distance between items. The icons were too big and needed to be changed to match with the names.
  4. The Organizations section should be removed because it repeats two times.
  5. A grey rectangle at “New Organizaition” should be deleted.
  6. At Recent files, it was a long scrollable list but it was hard for users to recognize.
  7. A “New Chart” button needed to be added.
  8. Quick Actions should be divided into two parts which are What’s New and Integrations.
  9. The white space was not balanced, it was hard for users to read.

FINAL DESIGN

The following showcases the after of my redesign, aloing with the explanations of my design choices:

1. Organization Name

I highlighted the organization name with organe-ish color from the color system.

This would allow users to be able to quickly recognize which organization they are watching or using.

2. Organization Section on the sidebar menu and Helps & Settings

After removing the Organization section, I moved Helps and Settings to the sidebar menu.

The lead designer wanted the top bar to be more clear and focus on “Upgrade Now” button. Besides, deleting the organization section will have more negative space.

3. Sidebar Menu Section

I changed some icons to match with the name along with its size. I moved the Help & Settings from the top bar to the menu bar. I also deleted the grey rectangle at the “New Organization” button.

The consistent space between the items are now more clear and structured.

4. Recent Files

The “New Chart” button was added as a shortcut for the users. A page number were moved to the bottom.

Instead of using the scrollable list, I used a clickable list that helps the users less stressed with data. And it’s also less negative space.

5. Quick Actions Section

It was divided into What’s New and Integrations.

The consistent space between the items is now more clear and structured.

6. Four main blocks with consistent structure

Dashboard, Recent Files, What’s New, and Integrations are now using the same style and size type with the consistent space between the top bar and themselves.

The dashboard is now well-arranged and clearer. The final design is simple and easy to navigate around the website.
