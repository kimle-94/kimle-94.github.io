overview

Foodie is a streaming platform for food lovers as well as a place where they can share tasty recipes. This project is a 4-days long design sprint submission for Twitch+Adobe Creative Jam, and I participated in a team of two UX/UI designers – myself included.

the challenge

Design a third-party desktop app or website that serves as a single-stop resource for any media related to a topic and encourages users to find new interests, events, and communities.

the project goal

During the Coronavirus pandemic, social interaction is severely restricted, particularly eating out. The current situation is that there is no streaming platform for food lovers even with the increasing number of users searching for recipes. In order to solve this problem, we need to design a community where foodies can find information about their passions in different ways and help each other connect the dots among multiple sources.

takeaways

Communication

This was the first competition that Bea and I had worked on together. At first, we were unsure where to begin since we have different schedules and work styles. We realized that the importance of communication would help us to understand our thoughts, feelings, and empathize with each other. Since we worked remotely via Whatsapp and Google Docs, we distributed our goals and tasks logically, based on our strengths, to remain on track each day.



A failure of user research

During this competition, Bea and I were approaching the end of our last semester at school. In addition, we had a time limit of 5 hours per day, which meant we could not conduct any user research such as user personas, interviews, or surveys. In this case, we took a step backward, as it was impossible for us to test our ideation and validate our solutions, such as user flows, the right way. This was a lesson for both Bea and me about the importance of user research.
